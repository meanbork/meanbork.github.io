Janky Raspberry Pi Surveillance Camera

Stuff I NEEDED: Raspberry Pi 3 B+, USB 2.0 extender male to female, Raspberry Pi camera V2 (I would use something nicer in the future - the max resolution is 8 mega pixels and ribbon cable blows).

Following are the steps I took to complete the project.

1: Install Raspbian Lite 2: After installing Lite, go ahead and Google “Raspbian Lite with RPD/LXDE/XFCE/MATE/i3/Openbox/X11 GUI”. Follow the steps and install the GUI you desire. 3: Restart. Open terminal. Type: ifconfig and jot down your IP. You are going to need it. 4: Type: sudo apt-get install motion 5: Check your camera's status. If you are using camera connected to USB port, Type: lsusb *** If you are using V2 or similar stuff, Type: raspistill -o test.jpg *** Not working? Type: sudo rasp-config, go to interface options and fiddle with the camera settings 6: Type: sudo nano /etc/motion/motion.conf *** daemon off -> daemon on *** width 320 -> 640 *** height 240 -> 480 *** framerate 2 -> 100 *** ffmpeg_output_movies on -> off (Turn it off so you stop recording & saving video files) *** stream_maxrate 1 -> 100 *** stream_localhost on -> off *** webcontrol_localhost on -> off *** save and exit motion.conf *** Tip: To look for keywords use :CTRL+W 7: Type: sudo nano /etc/default/motion *** Start_motion_daemon=no -> yes 8: Type: sudo nano /etc/rc.local *** Write in "sudo motion" 9: Type: sudo chmod 777 /var/lib/motion 10: This is going to depend on your router & ISP. I had to make a free account at "www.noip.com" to create DDNS 11: Either way - Go to your router and designate static IP (Your Raspberry Pi) for portforwarding. *** Internal port number = 8081. External port number = Your choice 12: Restart 13: Go to your browser (Any device with internet) and type in your address. Mine was meanbork.ddns.net:1234 Someone famous in Source Title There you have it. Your own janky surveillance camera.

Advisory: It is best to complete the DDNS process on your router and rotate port number. Also if you don't plan on changing camera's setting remotely, just leave the "Webcontrol_localhost on"
